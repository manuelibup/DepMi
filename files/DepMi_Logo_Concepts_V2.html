Category A — DM Monogram Icons

The "DM" Mark

Like how Facebook is "f", X is "𝕏", and Figma is "F" — these concepts turn DepMi's natural initials into a recognizable symbol.

★ TOP PICK

DM Fused

D and M share a vertical stroke — they overlap and interlock. One continuous mark. Reads as "DM" instantly. Clean, balanced, works at any size.

DM Stacked

D sits above M with a subtle diagonal energy line. Compact, vertical format — perfect for app icons where square space is tight.

CLEVER

M-in-D

The M lives INSIDE the D's counter space. A single unified shape — the D is the container, the M is the content. Negative space magic like FedEx's arrow.

DM Soft

Rounded, friendly, approachable. Feels like a social app (Instagram/Snapchat vibes) more than a corporate tool. Great for the younger demographic.

Category B — Pure Symbol Icons (No Letters)

Abstract & Symbolic Marks

Your dad's advice — just an image or character. These work without any text at all, like Apple's apple, Nike's swoosh, or Airbnb's Bélo.

★ TOP PICK

The Market Pin

"Buy HERE" — a location pin with a shopping bag inside. Literally says "DepMi" (buy here) in visual form. Maps + commerce. Universal and instantly understood.

The Exchange Loop

Two arrows flowing in a continuous circle with a dot at center — represents the endless cycle of buying and selling. Every transaction strengthens the community. Trust flows both ways.

The Spark

A lightning bolt — instant commerce, speed, energy. Like Flash's bolt or Gatorade's. Signals: "buying here is FAST." Bold, unmistakable silhouette at any size.

The Sprout

A seedling with two leaves — growth, nurturing, organic. Your business grows on DepMi. Connects to the Deps tier "Seedling" badge. Feels alive, natural, African.

AFRICAN

The Market Basket

A woven African market basket — the "udua" (market) in visual form. Deeply cultural. References the physical act of going to the market. Connects to Ibibio roots.

The Price Tag

A tilted price tag with a punched hole — universal symbol for "something for sale." Simple, clean, works at 16px favicon size. The circle is the "dep" dot.

The Buzz

A speech bubble with three dots — the conversation is happening. Commerce through social interaction. Represents the DM (direct message) in DepMi. Double meaning: DM = Direct Message = DepMi.

WELCOMING

The Open Door

"Buy HERE" — the door is open, come in. Light spills out invitingly. Represents DepMi opening the door for every vendor. Welcoming, warm, inclusive.

Category C — Hybrid (DM + Symbol)

DM Monogram Meets Concept

The DM letters embedded within a meaningful shape — getting the best of both worlds.

★ TOP PICK

DM Bag

Shopping bag with "DM" boldly inside. Combines the recognizable bag silhouette with the brand initials. Works like Chanel's "CC" inside their iconic shape.

DM Coin

DM inside a double-ringed coin — this IS the Dep. Every transaction mints one. The logo is literally the currency of trust on the platform.

DM Pin

"DM" inside a map pin — "Buy HERE" made visual. Location + brand in one mark. Like Google Maps pin but it's YOUR marketplace pin.

DM Shield

DM inside a shield — trust, protection, verification. Your money is safe here. Connects to the Deps credibility system and KYC verification tiers.

How To Pick Your Green

Each green sends a different signal:

#00C853 (Vibrant Green): Energetic, youthful, tech-forward. Says "this is a modern app." The current recommendation.
#00BFA5 (Teal Green): More sophisticated, less "financial." Feels like a lifestyle brand. Unique — nobody in Nigerian fintech uses teal-green.
#2E7D32 (Deep Forest): Premium, established, serious. More like a bank than a startup. Better for Phase 3+ when you want gravitas.
#1DE9B6 (Aqua Green): Fresh, youthful, eye-catching. Stands out from every competitor. But might feel too "techy" for market women.
#4CAF50 (Material Green): The safe middle ground. Trustworthy, calm, balanced. Won't stand out as much but won't alienate anyone.
#00E640 (Neon): Maximum energy. Impossible to ignore. Could feel overwhelming in a full UI though.

My recommendation stays at #00C853 — it's the sweet spot between energy and trust. But #00BFA5 (Teal) is my dark horse pick if you want something nobody else in Nigeria has.

My Top 3 Recommendations

🥇 #1 — The Market Pin (B1) or DM Bag (C1)

The Market Pin literally means "buy HERE" — it IS the DepMi concept in one image. The DM Bag is equally strong — it combines brand recognition (DM initials) with the universal commerce symbol (shopping bag). Either works globally. My advice: test both with 10 friends. Whichever one they remember after 24 hours is your logo.

🥈 #2 — M-in-D (A3)

The negative space concept is the most "designer" option — the M hidden inside the D is clever and becomes a talking point. "Did you notice the M is inside the D?" People love discovering hidden meanings in logos (FedEx arrow, Amazon smile). But it requires a skilled designer to execute perfectly.

🥉 #3 — The Market Basket (B5)

If you want to lean HARD into the African identity, the woven market basket is unmatched. No tech company has a basket logo. It's distinctive, cultural, and tells a story. The risk: it might feel "traditional" rather than "tech" to some users. But Ubuntu proved African symbolism works globally.

These are directional concepts — take your top 2-3 favorites to a professional designer (Fiverr: $50-$200, 99designs: $300-$800, local Nigerian designer: ₦50K-₦200K) for pixel-perfect refinement, proper vector files (SVG, AI, EPS), and all the size variants you'll need (app icon, favicon, social avatar, print).
